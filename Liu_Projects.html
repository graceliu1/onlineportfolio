DPIE Science Fair 2018
***THIS PROJECT IS BEING DEVELOPED***

Over the summer, I took Biology with Research at a Dublin Partners In Education program (DPIE). I participated in the science fair with Andrea Jia and our project was titled "The Effect of Luminol Concentration and Temperature on Chemiluminescence." ~Our science fair project focused on the topic of luminescence and the best conditions in which light could endure. We were first inspired by the topic of bioluminescence, the natural emission of light in living organisms, and the scientific application of this to our current needs. Due to our inability to obtain actual bioluminescent organisms, we decided to model the process with chemiluminescence, the production of light through chemical reactions. For all intents and purposes, the reactions are very similar. ~The above is a direct excerpt from the final report submitted to Megan Sininger at the Science Fair.

Space Invaders
September 2018-Early October 2018, Computer Science Principles

This project was part of my Computer Science Principles class and spanned about three weeks. The objective was to design a successful app using Massachusetts Institute of Technology App Inventor software. My role in the project was a documentor and programmer. My partner was Edward He. At the beginning of this project, we chose a tutorial from the website and developed it further. The primary objective of our final game was to win by gaining 25 points and complete three different levels. To make the game interactive, we added the features of a health bar, self-firing auto sprites, and a reset button. Some challenges we faced during this project were successfully creating an auto sprite that could fire back with any glitches. We were able to get feedback on our game from two seperate gallery walks where other classmates reviewed us. Overall, this project taught me important skills about using a new type of coding "language." In addition, I gained team experience by working with a partner.
