Honors Principles of Engineering 2019-2020


Solar Hydrogen Vehicle
November 2019, Honors Principles of Engineering

This project was part of my Principles of Engineering class and spanned about three weeks. The goal was to design a scale prototype with power from solar cells or hydrogen fuel cells and make a reconmendation to Tesla. My teammates were Suhani Bhakta, Kauri Mansfield, and Nirupama Suravarjjala. My role in the project was working on our online notebook and being one of the main builders on our team. I worked with my group members to come up with the final design, which was a car built with a single steel c-channel and powered by various configurations of solar and hydrogen fuel cells connected to a breadboard and motor. The motor only powered a single wheel, but successfully moved our car. Another contribution I made to the project was wiring the different configurations on our car, with both parallel and series circuits. We ultimately chose to recommend hydrogen as the power source due to its higher reliability. Although solar power was usually faster, there was a chance of damaged solar panels affecting the performance of our car. After finishing the build, I was responsible for creating the modification logs on our documentation.

West Point Bridge Designer
March 2020, Honors Principles of Engineering

This project took place during the second semester of my Principles of Engineering class. My partner was Erika Tran. The goal was to design a bridge using West Point Bridge Designer under several specified constraints, such as cost and stability. My main role in the project was developing two specific designs and modifying them to create the ideal bridge. I also worked on documentation. Although we started with four possible bridge designs, we were able to narrow our options down to one after using a decision matrix. The three characteristics used to determine the final bridge design were stability, aesthetics, and cost. The final bridge design consisted of a modified truss with a pier in the center of the river. This bridge design successfully minimized the forces of tension and compression, which resulted in a stable bridge. The deck elevation was 12 meters and used high-strength concrete The overall cost was under $300,000.00. I also contributed to our final presentation. Finally, I was responsible for creating the final design summary and parts of the modification logs.
