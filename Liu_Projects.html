Space Invaders
September 2018, Computer Science Principles

This project was part of my Computer Science Principles class and spanned about three weeks. The objective was to design a successful app using Massachusetts Institute of Technology App Inventor software. My role in the project was documenting in our online notebook and programming. My partner was Edward He. At the beginning of this project, we chose a tutorial from the website and developed it further. The primary objective of our final game was to win by gaining 25 points and complete three different levels. To make the game interactive, we added the features of a health bar, self-firing auto sprites, and a reset button. Some challenges we faced during this project were successfully creating an auto sprite that could fire back with any glitches. We were able to get feedback on our game from two seperate gallery walks where other classmates reviewed us. Overall, this project taught me important skills about using a new type of coding "language." In addition, I gained team experience by working with a partner.

Family Framers
April 2019, Computer Science Principles

During second semester of the 2018-2019, I worked on an image manipulation project with my partner Megna Sankaranarayan. This project was coded in Python and provided a variety of different manipulations that could be applied to inputted images. Our topic was focused on family, so our project brand was titled "Family Framers." In the beginning, we brainstormed different ideas on what we wanted our final product to look like. We came up with the idea of having the customers choose from a variety of options. Later, we also added a color parameter to all our frames so the user could choose how they wanted the frame to look. We created six different manipulations, among them including black and white, abstract, and cutout shapes. I developed a large portion of the code for this project. This project also helped learn how to make efficient use of the Python Image Library (PIL) and numpy manipulations. Even though I focused on coding this project, I also worked with my partner to collaborate on documentation. At the end of the project, we created an online presentation to demonstrate our final product.
